Bio

Hello! I'm Teresa LaBolle, a software developer who's happiest working on frontend design and crafting interfaces that feel both intuitive and delightful. I still care deeply about writing thoughtful, well-tested software, and I've worked on everything from automated testing and backend services to hardware-adjacent projects. However, I especially enjoy web development and UI work, where code and visual design intersect. Outside of tech, you'll most likely find me hiking, crocheting, painting, diving into video games, or tackling a home-improvement project. Before moving into software, I also worked as a Mandarin medical interpreter and florist; these experiences taught me how to balance creativity with precision, listen carefully, and communicate clearly with the people I'm working with.

Previous Work

Software developer and SDET intern with experience in UI test automation, developer tooling, and backend scripting. At The Standard, I design and execute end-to-end integration tests for Online Evidence of Insurability (EOI), build Cypress UI workflows, and automate multi-step manual tasks into single-run processes. At Portland State University, I maintained a Python-based student check-in system, wrote SQL migrations from PostgreSQL to MySQL, and supported students in the Electronics Prototyping Lab while also leading communications and outreach for the Maseeh College. Previous work on OreSat cubesat software, C++ tutoring, and Mandarin medical interpreting has strengthened my debugging and cross-functional communication skills.
